Intelligence Committee to begin circulating draft Ukraine report Monday

by MELANIE ZANONA, KYLE CHENEY and HEATHER CAYGLE

Members of the House Intelligence Committee will begin reviewing a report Monday on the panel’s investigation of President Donald Trump’s efforts to press Ukraine to investigate his Democratic political rivals, a crucial step in the impeachment process.

Lawmakers on the panel will get a 24-hour review period, according to internal guidance sent to committee members and obtained by POLITICO. On Tuesday, the Intelligence Committee will vote on the report’s transmission to the House Judiciary Committee, which is expected to conduct impeachment hearings starting next week.

Intelligence Committee Chairman Adam Schiff had indicated in a letter to colleagues earlier this week that a report would be coming “soon” from his committee but had not provided a specific timeline.
